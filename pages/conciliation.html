Conciliation

Independent conciliation combining facilitative and evaluative approaches for effective dispute resolution.

Facilitative and Evaluative Resolution

Conciliation occupies a distinct position within the spectrum of alternative dispute resolution mechanisms. Unlike mediation, where the neutral's role is confined to facilitating negotiation between the parties, conciliation permits the conciliator to take a more active role in the resolution process. The conciliator may investigate the facts, assess the merits of each party's position, and propose terms of settlement for the parties' consideration.

IDRS conciliation services combine facilitative and evaluative approaches, drawing upon deep technical expertise in construction, engineering, and commercial matters. The conciliator's ability to provide an informed assessment of the issues and suggest settlement terms where appropriate can materially assist parties in reaching resolution, particularly in technically complex disputes where an independent expert perspective may bridge the gap between the parties' positions.

The Conciliation Process

The conciliator engages with both parties to understand the dispute, investigate the relevant facts, and identify the issues requiring resolution. Unlike arbitration, the process is non-adversarial and focuses on achieving a negotiated outcome that reflects the commercial interests of the parties. The conciliator may meet with the parties jointly and individually, conduct site inspections, review documentary evidence, and consult with technical experts as the circumstances warrant.

Settlement Proposals

Where the conciliator forms a view on the merits or quantum of the dispute, they may prepare and present settlement proposals for the parties' consideration. These proposals are informed by the conciliator's technical expertise and assessment of the evidence, but are not binding on the parties. The parties retain the right to accept, reject, or negotiate modifications to any proposed terms.

Technical Assessment

The conciliator's construction and engineering expertise enables informed evaluation of technical matters including quantum, delay, defects, and contractual interpretation.

Confidentiality

All conciliation proceedings are confidential. Proposals made and positions adopted during the process cannot be disclosed or relied upon in subsequent proceedings.

Preservation of Relationships

The non-adversarial nature of conciliation supports the preservation of commercial relationships, an important consideration in the construction industry where ongoing contractual obligations may continue beyond the dispute.

International Availability

IDRS conciliation services are available across Australia, Asia, and the United Kingdom, with familiarity across multiple jurisdictional and contractual frameworks.
