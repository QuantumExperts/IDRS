About IDRS

Independent dispute resolution expertise for the Asia-Pacific construction and infrastructure sectors.

International Dispute Resolution Services

IDRS — International Dispute Resolution Services — delivers independent dispute resolution services for construction, infrastructure, resources, and engineering sectors across Australia, Asia, and the United Kingdom. The practice is focused exclusively on dispute resolution, providing arbitration, mediation, expert determination, conciliation, dispute board, and adjudication services with a deep understanding of both the technical and commercial dimensions of the matters in which it is engaged.

The practice applies a combination of technical expertise and legal acumen to each engagement, drawing upon combined decades of construction and engineering experience to bridge technical complexity with the requirements of the legal process. This dual capability enables IDRS to provide efficient, well-informed dispute resolution across a wide range of forums and jurisdictional contexts.

IDRS maintains accredited panels of practitioners across multiple jurisdictions including Australia, Thailand, Vietnam, India, Sri Lanka, China, and the broader Asia-Pacific region. All panel members hold relevant professional qualifications, complete specialised training, and adhere to a comprehensive Code of Professional and Ethical Conduct with continuing professional development requirements.

Neil Kirkpatrick

IDRS is led by Neil Kirkpatrick, Managing Director, a Chartered Quantity Surveyor, Chartered Construction Manager, Arbitrator, Adjudicator, Expert Determiner, Mediator, and Expert Witness with over twenty-five years of specialist experience in construction disputes. Neil's practice encompasses quantum, technical matters, and delay analysis related to large-scale development, construction projects, and disputes across Australia, Asia, and the United Kingdom, including disputes valued in excess of $150 million involving testimony in international arbitration and court proceedings.

Academic Qualifications

Neil holds a Master of Laws (LL.M.) in International Commercial Law with Distinction, a Diploma in International Commercial Arbitration (DipICArb), and a Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Construction, Engineering and Management. He also holds certificates in adjudication and mediation, and has completed the Academy of Experts Expert Witness Training Program in Hong Kong.

Chartered Fellowships

Neil is a Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (FCIArb) through the International Arbitration Pathway and is a Business Arbitration Panel Member (London). He is a Fellow of the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (FRICS) and sits on the Dispute Resolution Faculty as an Arbitrator, Mediator, and Adjudicator. He is a Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Building (FCIOB) as a Chartered Construction Manager and Builder, and a Fellow of the Resolution Institute (FRI) where he serves as Chair of the NSW Determinative Committee and sits on the Expert Witness and Expert Determination Committees. These fellowships represent the highest grade of membership in each institution and reflect sustained contribution to the relevant disciplines over many years.

Dispute Resolution Accreditations

Neil is a registered adjudicator in Western Australia, New South Wales, Queensland, and the Northern Territory. He is a Resolution Institute Accredited Arbitrator (Grade 2), an accredited expert determiner, expert witness, and mediator through both the Resolution Institute and the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors. He holds the Advanced Dispute Board Member Certificate from the Dispute Resolution Board Foundation and is recognised across multiple panels and appointing bodies in the Asia-Pacific region.

Professional Memberships & Appointments

Neil is a Founding Member and Chairman of the Construction Arbitration Centre Asia Pacific (CACAP), a specialist independent dispute resolution institution providing comprehensive dispute resolution services for construction and infrastructure disputes across the Asia-Pacific region. He is Chair of the Resolution Institute Expert Witness National Training and Accreditation Program, a National Committee Member and Presenter for the Resolution Institute Procedural Fairness and Expert Determination Program, a member of the Society of Construction Law, and a member of the Dispute Resolution Board Foundation (Region 3). Neil also serves as a university lecturer on dispute resolution.

Jurisdictions

Neil's practice extends across New South Wales, Western Australia, Queensland, the Northern Territory, Victoria, the Australian Capital Territory, Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, Indonesia, England and Wales, Scotland, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, Fiji, and the United Arab Emirates.

Sectors

Neil's experience encompasses building, engineering, infrastructure, marine, and resource industries, with particular expertise in major infrastructure projects across rail, road, water, and energy sectors.
