Dispute Boards

Proactive dispute management through standing and ad hoc boards for construction and infrastructure projects.

Early Intervention, Continuous Engagement

Dispute boards provide a dedicated mechanism for the proactive management and resolution of disputes arising during the performance of construction and infrastructure contracts. Unlike reactive dispute resolution processes such as arbitration or litigation, dispute boards are established at the commencement of a project and operate continuously throughout the contract period, enabling early identification and resolution of disagreements before they escalate into formal disputes.

IDRS provides experienced dispute board members with deep construction industry knowledge, combining technical expertise in quantum, delay, and contractual administration with a thorough understanding of the procedural requirements of dispute board practice under both FIDIC and bespoke contractual arrangements.

Dispute Adjudication Boards (DABs)

DABs issue decisions that are binding on the parties and must be complied with immediately, even if a party provides notice of dissatisfaction. The binding nature of DAB decisions provides certainty and prevents disputes from delaying project progress, whilst preserving each party's right to seek final resolution through arbitration if they remain dissatisfied with the decision.

Dispute Review Boards (DRBs)

DRBs issue recommendations rather than binding decisions. Whilst the parties are not obliged to accept a DRB recommendation, in practice the persuasive authority of recommendations issued by experienced industry professionals encourages settlement in the vast majority of cases.

Combined Dispute Boards (CDBs)

The combined model, adopted in the FIDIC 2017 suite, enables the board to issue both decisions and recommendations depending on the nature and circumstances of the referral. This flexibility allows the board to select the most appropriate intervention for each dispute, whether that is a binding decision requiring immediate compliance or a non-binding recommendation facilitating negotiated settlement.

Standing Boards

Established at project commencement and maintained throughout the contract period, enabling regular site visits, continuous familiarity with the project, and rapid intervention when disagreements arise.

Ad Hoc Boards

Constituted to address specific disputes as they arise, providing a cost-effective alternative for projects where a standing board may not be warranted.

FIDIC Compatibility

Full familiarity with FIDIC dispute resolution provisions, including the 1999 DAB procedures and the 2017 DAAB framework with its avoidance and adjudication functions.

Dispute Avoidance

Informal assistance and advisory opinions to help parties resolve disagreements before they crystallise into formal disputes, preserving commercial relationships.
