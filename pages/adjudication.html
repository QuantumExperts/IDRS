Adjudication & Security of Payment

Statutory adjudication and payment dispute resolution across Australian jurisdictions.

Rapid Resolution of Payment Disputes

Security of payment legislation provides a statutory right to progress payments for construction work and related goods and services. Where payment disputes arise, adjudication offers a rapid, interim dispute resolution mechanism designed to maintain cash flow throughout the construction process. IDRS provides adjudication, expert quantum analysis, and valuation services under the security of payment legislation of multiple Australian states and territories.

The adjudication process is characterised by its speed and enforceability. Determinations are made within compressed timeframes prescribed by the relevant legislation, and adjudication determinations are enforceable as debts due under the applicable statutes. This interim binding nature ensures that payment disputes do not impede the progress of construction works whilst preserving the parties' rights to have the underlying dispute finally determined through arbitration, expert determination, or litigation.

Adjudicator Appointments

IDRS accepts appointments as adjudicator under the security of payment legislation in Western Australia, New South Wales, Queensland, and the Northern Territory. Neil Kirkpatrick is a registered adjudicator in each of these jurisdictions, with extensive experience in the application of the relevant legislative schemes to complex construction payment disputes.

Jurisdictional Frameworks

Western Australia

The Construction Contracts Act 2004 (WA) establishes the statutory adjudication framework for construction payment disputes in Western Australia. IDRS provides adjudication, expert quantum analysis, and valuation services under this Act, which applies to all construction contracts for construction work carried out in Western Australia. The Act provides for rapid adjudication of payment disputes with determinations typically required within fourteen business days of acceptance of the application.

New South Wales

The Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW) provides the legislative framework for adjudication of payment claims in New South Wales. IDRS is registered under this Act and offers adjudication, valuation, and claim support services. The NSW scheme operates on a progress payment basis, with strict timeframes governing the service of payment claims, provision of payment schedules, and the making of adjudication applications.

Queensland

The Building Industry Fairness (Security of Payment) Act 2017 (Qld) governs security of payment adjudication in Queensland. IDRS assists with progress claims, variations, and payment schedule disputes under this legislation. The Queensland scheme incorporates specific provisions for project trust accounts and retention trust accounts alongside the adjudication mechanism, reflecting the state's comprehensive approach to payment security in the construction industry.

Northern Territory

The Construction Contracts (Security of Payments) Act 2004 (NT) establishes the adjudication framework for the Northern Territory. IDRS provides adjudication and expert analysis for payment disputes under this Act. The Northern Territory scheme applies to construction contracts for work carried out in the Territory, with provisions governing progress payments, payment disputes, and the appointment and powers of adjudicators.

International Adjudication

Beyond the Australian statutory schemes, IDRS also has experience with construction adjudication frameworks in other jurisdictions, including the United Kingdom's Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996, Singapore's Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act, and Malaysia's Construction Industry Payment and Adjudication Act 2012 (CIPAA). This cross-jurisdictional experience enables IDRS to advise on and participate in adjudication proceedings across the Asia-Pacific region and beyond.
