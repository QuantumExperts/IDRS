Jurisdictional Considerations

Jurisdictions

Dispute resolution frameworks and institutional considerations across the Asia-Pacific region and beyond.

International Dispute Resolution Practice

IDRS provides dispute resolution services across sixteen jurisdictions spanning the Asia-Pacific, Europe, and North America. Services include commercial arbitration, expert determination, mediation, security of payment adjudication, dispute boards, expedited arbitration, and conciliation. Each jurisdiction is governed by distinct legislative frameworks, institutional rules, and procedural requirements, and an understanding of the applicable legal and institutional landscape is essential to the effective conduct of any dispute resolution process.

Select a jurisdiction below to view the detailed legislative frameworks, institutional considerations, and available dispute resolution mechanisms.

Asia-Pacific Jurisdictions

Australia
Comprehensive federal and state frameworks including the International Arbitration Act 1974 (Cth), ACICA Rules, Commercial Arbitration Acts across all states and territories, and security of payment legislation governing construction dispute adjudication.
View jurisdiction guide →
Hong Kong
A leading international arbitration seat governed by the Arbitration Ordinance (Cap. 609), HKIAC Administered Arbitration Rules, and Mediation Ordinance (Cap. 620), with strong judicial support for arbitral autonomy and enforcement.
View jurisdiction guide →
Singapore
Premier Asia-Pacific arbitration hub under the International Arbitration Act (Cap. 143A), SIAC Rules, Singapore International Mediation Centre, and the Singapore Convention on Mediation facilitating cross-border enforcement.
View jurisdiction guide →
Malaysia
Rapidly developing framework under the Arbitration Act 2005, AIAC Arbitration Rules, Construction Industry Payment and Adjudication Act 2012 (CIPAA), and Mediation Act 2012 supporting statutory adjudication.
View jurisdiction guide →
China
Governed by the Arbitration Law 1994 and administered through CIETAC and BAC, with evolving mechanisms for Belt and Road dispute resolution and increasing engagement with international arbitration standards.
View jurisdiction guide →
India
Substantial reforms under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996 (as amended), with MCIA and DIAC providing institutional frameworks, complemented by the Commercial Courts Act 2015 for expedited enforcement.
View jurisdiction guide →
Indonesia
Framework established by Law No. 30 of 1999 on Arbitration and Alternative Dispute Resolution, with BANI providing institutional administration and the Construction Services Law 2017 governing sector-specific disputes.
View jurisdiction guide →
Japan
UNCITRAL Model Law jurisdiction under the Arbitration Act 2003, with JCAA and JIDRC providing institutional frameworks, and specialist Construction Dispute Review Committees for infrastructure projects.
View jurisdiction guide →
Philippines
ADR Act of 2004 (RA 9285) providing a comprehensive framework, with PDRCI administering commercial arbitration and CIAC exercising exclusive jurisdiction over construction disputes under Executive Order 1008.
View jurisdiction guide →
South Korea
Modern arbitration framework under the Korean Arbitration Act, with KCAB International Arbitration Rules providing institutional administration and established construction dispute resolution mechanisms.
View jurisdiction guide →
Taiwan
Arbitration Act providing a comprehensive framework, with the Chinese Arbitration Association administering proceedings and the Public Construction Commission overseeing government infrastructure dispute mechanisms.
View jurisdiction guide →
Thailand
Arbitration Act B.E. 2545 (2002) aligned with UNCITRAL Model Law principles, with the Thailand Arbitration Center (THAC) and Thai Arbitration Institute providing institutional administration for commercial disputes.
View jurisdiction guide →
Vietnam
Law on Commercial Arbitration 2010 establishing a modern framework, with VIAC providing institutional arbitration services and the Construction Law 2014 governing sector-specific dispute resolution procedures.
View jurisdiction guide →

Europe & North America

United Kingdom
Globally pre-eminent arbitration seat under the Arbitration Act 1996, with LCIA and ICC institutional rules, statutory adjudication under the Housing Grants Act 1996, and the Technology and Construction Court providing specialist judicial oversight.
View jurisdiction guide →
United States
Federal Arbitration Act 1925 establishing a strong pro-arbitration policy, with AAA-ICDR and JAMS providing institutional administration, and Dispute Resolution Boards widely utilised for major infrastructure projects.
View jurisdiction guide →
Canada
Federal and provincial arbitration legislation including the Federal Commercial Arbitration Act, ADR Institute of Canada Rules, and emerging prompt payment legislation for the construction sector.
