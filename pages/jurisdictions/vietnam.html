Jurisdictional Considerations

Vietnam

Dispute Resolution Framework

Vietnam has undergone substantial economic development in recent decades and has developed a dispute resolution framework supporting its rapid economic growth and increasing international commercial engagement. The Vietnamese dispute resolution landscape encompasses arbitration, mediation, conciliation, and litigation, underpinned by statutory frameworks reflecting communist legal traditions combined with modern amendments recognising international commercial dispute resolution mechanisms. Vietnam's transformation from a centrally-planned to a market-oriented economy has necessitated the development of modern dispute resolution frameworks supporting international commercial engagement and investment attraction.

The Vietnamese approach to dispute resolution reflects the jurisdiction's effort to balance commitment to governmental oversight of economic activity with recognition of the need for efficient commercial dispute resolution mechanisms. The dispute resolution framework includes arbitration legislation recognising international commercial arbitration, the Vietnam International Arbitration Centre (VIAC) operating as a leading arbitration institution, and developing mediation frameworks. Vietnam's role as an increasingly important centre for manufacturing, technology development, and international investment has established the jurisdiction as an important seat for regional arbitration and commercial dispute resolution.

Arbitration Legislation

The principal arbitration legislation in Vietnam is the Law on Commercial Arbitration 2010 (Law 54/2010/QH12), which establishes the legal framework for commercial arbitration in Vietnam. This legislation provides the statutory foundation for arbitration and incorporates principles reflecting the UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration. The Law addresses arbitration agreements, arbitrator appointment and qualifications, conduct of arbitral proceedings, interim relief, awards, and enforcement mechanisms. The legislation provides that arbitration agreements are valid and enforceable and establishes procedures for arbitration aligned with contemporary international practice.

The Law on Commercial Arbitration 2010 represents a significant modernisation of Vietnam's arbitration legal framework and reflects the jurisdiction's commitment to supporting international commercial arbitration as the economy becomes increasingly integrated into international commerce. The legislation provides broad freedom of contract regarding procedural matters and establishes that arbitrators may determine their own jurisdiction. The legislation emphasises the finality of arbitral awards and limits grounds for challenging awards, reflecting contemporary approaches to arbitration law.

The Vietnamese courts have generally adopted a supportive approach to arbitration and have demonstrated restraint in interfering with arbitral proceedings and awards. This judicial approach has contributed to Vietnam's attractiveness as an arbitration seat and has supported the development of arbitration practice within the jurisdiction. The courts' application of the Law on Commercial Arbitration reflects recognition of the importance of arbitration to Vietnam's economic development and international commercial engagement.

Institutional Frameworks

The Vietnam International Arbitration Centre (VIAC) serves as Vietnam's primary arbitration institution and operates the VIAC Arbitration Rules. VIAC administers commercial arbitrations and maintains headquarters in Ho Chi Minh City with an office in Hanoi. The VIAC Rules provide comprehensive procedures for administered arbitrations and address all aspects of the arbitration process from initiation through award issuance. VIAC has positioned itself as Vietnam's leading arbitration provider and has worked to develop institutional capacity and procedures supporting international commercial arbitration.

VIAC maintains a roster of arbitrators with expertise spanning commercial, construction, technology, engineering, and other fields. The Centre provides administrative services for arbitrations, including assistance with arbitrator appointment, hearing logistics, and award procedures. VIAC has developed relationships with international arbitration practitioners and has promoted Vietnam as an arbitration seat for regional and international disputes involving Vietnamese parties or relating to Vietnam. The institution's development has contributed to Vietnam's visibility in the international arbitration marketplace and has supported the development of arbitration practice within Vietnam.

Beyond VIAC, dispute resolution services are provided by private arbitrators and alternative dispute resolution providers. The availability of alternative providers creates a competitive marketplace in which parties may select providers suited to their dispute resolution requirements and preferences.

Mediation Frameworks

Mediation is recognised in Vietnam as an alternative dispute resolution mechanism and is promoted through institutional frameworks and government policy. The Vietnamese dispute resolution system includes mediation as a formal mechanism and mediation services are provided through various institutional and private providers. Mediation is increasingly utilised in commercial disputes and is promoted as an efficient alternative to arbitration and litigation. The availability of mediation services and government support for mediation have contributed to increased utilisation of mediation in Vietnam's dispute resolution landscape.

Institutional mediation services are provided by VIAC and various other organisations offering mediation services. Mediators are trained in mediation techniques and are drawn from diverse professional backgrounds. The availability of institutional mediation services has supported the utilisation of mediation as an alternative to arbitration and litigation. The development of a professional mediation community in Vietnam reflects the increasing importance of mediation in the jurisdiction's dispute resolution framework.

The Vietnamese courts have increasingly promoted mediation and alternative dispute resolution as mechanisms for achieving settlement and reducing court congestion. The courts' support for mediation reflects governmental policy encouraging consensual dispute resolution and reflects recognition of the benefits of settlement in reducing court burden and improving access to justice.

Construction Law and Dispute Resolution

Vietnam has enacted the Construction Law 2014 (amended 2020), which establishes the legal framework for regulation of the construction industry and addresses matters including construction contracting, contractor licensing, quality standards, and dispute resolution. The legislation recognises arbitration and mediation as appropriate mechanisms for resolving construction disputes and provides that construction disputes may be referred to arbitration. The legislation reflects Vietnam's recognition that the construction industry requires efficient dispute resolution mechanisms and acknowledges the importance of arbitration to construction dispute resolution.

The construction industry in Vietnam utilises arbitration through VIAC, mediation, and contractual dispute resolution mechanisms for addressing disputes. The use of Dispute Boards and other interim determination mechanisms is increasingly incorporated into major construction contracts, particularly those involving international contractors or international financing. These mechanisms enable rapid resolution of interim disputes whilst construction proceeds, protecting project schedule and cash flow. The integration of dispute resolution mechanisms into construction contracts reflects international best practice and is increasingly common in major Vietnamese construction projects.

VIAC has developed expertise in construction dispute resolution and operates procedures addressing construction disputes. The Commission's experience in resolving construction disputes has established it as a capable forum for construction dispute resolution and reflects Vietnam's development of specialised capabilities in this important sector.

Expert Determination

Expert determination is recognised in Vietnam as a mechanism for resolving disputes involving technical or specialised matters. Expert determinations may be conducted pursuant to contractual frameworks or through institutional arrangements. The professional services sector in Vietnam includes expert determiners with expertise in construction engineering, architecture, property matters, commercial valuation, and other specialised fields. The availability of qualified expert determiners has supported the utilisation of expert determination for resolving technical disputes in construction and commercial contexts.

The Vietnamese courts have recognised expert determinations as valid mechanisms for addressing disputes and have generally enforced determinations made within the expert's jurisdiction and in accordance with procedural fairness requirements. This judicial recognition of expert determination has encouraged its utilisation and has provided parties with confidence that determinations made by qualified experts will be recognised and enforced by the courts.

Enforcement and Recognition

Vietnam is a signatory to the Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards 1958 and awards made in foreign jurisdictions are enforceable in Vietnam. The procedures for enforcement of foreign awards are established in the Law on Commercial Arbitration 2010 and in rules of court procedure. The Vietnamese courts have adopted a generally supportive approach to enforcement of foreign awards, subject to compliance with Vietnamese law and public policy. The courts maintain limited grounds for refusing enforcement consistent with the New York Convention requirements.

Awards made in Vietnamese arbitrations are enforceable through the Vietnamese courts. The enforcement process requires application to the court with jurisdiction over the party against whom enforcement is sought. The courts may refuse enforcement only where enforcement would violate public policy or where the arbitration was conducted in violation of fundamental procedural requirements. The Vietnamese courts' approach to arbitration enforcement emphasises the finality of arbitral awards and limits judicial intervention in arbitral matters.

Mediation and conciliation settlements are enforceable as settlement agreements and may be enforced through court procedures. The statutory framework supports the enforceability of alternative dispute resolution outcomes and provides parties with confidence regarding the legal effect of agreements reached through mediation and other consensual mechanisms.

Conclusion

Vietnam has developed a comprehensive dispute resolution framework reflecting its economic transformation and its increasing integration into the international economy. The Law on Commercial Arbitration 2010, the development of VIAC as a leading regional arbitration institution, the development of mediation frameworks, and the integration of dispute resolution mechanisms into the Construction Law 2014 demonstrate Vietnam's comprehensive approach to dispute resolution. Vietnam's role as an increasingly important manufacturing and technology centre and its status as a major recipient of foreign investment have established effective dispute resolution mechanisms as important to commercial confidence and investment attraction. For parties seeking to resolve disputes involving Vietnamese parties or relating to projects or transactions in Vietnam, the jurisdiction provides modern legal frameworks, established institutional infrastructure, and a developing professional dispute resolution community supporting effective and efficient dispute resolution.
