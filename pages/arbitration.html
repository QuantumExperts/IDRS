Commercial Arbitration

Streamlined arbitration delivering binding, enforceable awards for construction and commercial disputes.

Efficient, Binding Resolution

IDRS provides commercial arbitration services for construction, engineering, and infrastructure disputes. The arbitration process delivers final, binding awards that are enforceable internationally under the New York Convention, providing parties with certainty of outcome and global enforceability.

The practice applies deep construction industry expertise to proceedings, ensuring that technical matters are understood at a granular level. Fixed-cost schemes are available for disputes of limited complexity, providing cost certainty alongside procedural rigour.

Construction Industry Focus

With over two decades of experience in construction dispute resolution, IDRS brings substantive understanding of quantum, delay, disruption, defective work, and contractual administration to arbitral proceedings. This technical competence enables more efficient proceedings and more informed awards.

Procedural Flexibility

Each arbitration is conducted in accordance with the rules agreed between the parties, whether institutional rules (SIAC, HKIAC, ACICA, ICC) or ad hoc arrangements under the UNCITRAL Rules. The process is tailored to the complexity, value, and jurisdictional requirements of each dispute.

Sole Arbitrator

Specialist construction expertise ensures technical understanding, with efficient case management and a single point of contact for procedural matters.

Binding Awards

Final determinations enforceable internationally under the New York Convention, providing parties with certainty and global reach.

Cost Efficiency

Fixed-cost schemes for limited-complexity disputes and proportionate fee structures that reflect the value and complexity of each matter.

Due Process

Full procedural safeguards maintained throughout, preserving parties' rights to present evidence and cross-examine witnesses.

Expedited Arbitration

IDRS offers an expedited arbitration procedure designed for disputes of moderate value or complexity where parties require efficient resolution. The procedure is suitable for disputes not exceeding USD 1,000,000 or where the parties agree to expedited procedures regardless of the amount in dispute.

Compressed Timetable

Final awards are issued within six months of arbitrator appointment, with the possibility of extension to nine months in exceptional circumstances. Defined procedural milestones provide certainty of process and ensure that neither party is disadvantaged by the compressed timeframe.

Proportionate Resolution

The expedited framework maintains full due process safeguards whilst eliminating unnecessary procedural delay. Reduced administrative fees reflect the proportionality principle, ensuring that the cost of resolution bears a reasonable relationship to the value of the dispute.
